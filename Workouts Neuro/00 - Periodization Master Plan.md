# 12-Month Periodization Plan

## Overview

This program uses **block periodization** with interchangeable components. Each 4-week mesocycle focuses on specific adaptations while maintaining overall fitness.

## Annual Structure

| Phase | Duration | Focus | Intensity | Volume |
|-------|----------|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Phase 1: Foundation** | Weeks 1-8 | Movement quality, base strength | Low-Moderate | High |
| **Phase 2: Strength** | Weeks 9-16 | Max strength, power | Moderate-High | Moderate |
| **Phase 3: Power/Speed** | Weeks 17-24 | Explosive power, conditioning | High | Low-Moderate |
| **Phase 4: Peak/Maintain** | Weeks 25-32 | Performance, skill work | Variable | Moderate |
| **Phase 5: Hypertrophy** | Weeks 33-40 | Muscle building, strength-endurance | Moderate | High |
| **Phase 6: Active Recovery** | Weeks 41-48 | Recovery, mobility, light conditioning | Low | Low |

---

## Weekly Template

### Standard Training Week (3-4 days)

| Day | Focus | Duration |
|-----|-------|----------|
| **Day 1** | Lower Body Strength + Conditioning | 60 min |
| **Day 2** | Upper Body Strength + Core | 60 min |
| **Day 3** | Full Body Power + Metabolic | 45-60 min |
| **Day 4** (Optional) | Active Recovery / Mobility | 30-45 min |

---

## Workout Structure Template

Every workout follows this structure:

```
1. ACTIVE WARMUP (5-10 min)
   - Cardio activation (Row, Bike, Jump Rope)
   - Movement prep

2. DYNAMIC MOBILITY (10 min)
   - Movement-specific stretches
   - Activation drills

3. STRENGTH BLOCK (20-25 min)
   - Primary lift (compound)
   - Accessory work
   - Rest/mobility between sets

4. CONDITIONING BLOCK (15-20 min)
   - Format varies by phase (AMRAP, EMOM, Rounds)
   - Matches phase goals

5. FINISHER/COOLDOWN (5-10 min)
   - Core work or cashout
   - Foam roll / stretch
```

## Phase Details

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-8)

**Goals:**
- Establish movement patterns
- Build work capacity
- Address mobility limitations

**Strength Focus:**
- Goblet Squats, Split Squats
- Push Ups, TRX Rows
- Deadlift patterns (RDL, KB DL)
- TGU progression

**Conditioning Format:**
- Longer AMRAPs (15-20 min)
- Lower intensity
- Focus on form over speed

**Rep Ranges:**
- Strength: 3-4 sets x 10-12 reps
- Conditioning: Higher reps, moderate load

---

### Phase 2: Strength (Weeks 9-16)

**Goals:**
- Increase max strength
- Progressive overload
- Maintain conditioning base

**Strength Focus:**
- Back Squat / Front Squat
- Bench Press / Overhead Press
- Deadlift (conventional/sumo)
- Pull Ups / Rows

**Conditioning Format:**
- Shorter, intense intervals
- EMOMs (8-12 min)
- Strength-biased circuits

**Rep Ranges:**
- Strength: 4-5 sets x 5-8 reps
- Build to heavy singles/doubles on test weeks

---

### Phase 3: Power/Speed (Weeks 17-24)

**Goals:**
- Develop explosive power
- Improve rate of force development
- Peak conditioning

**Strength Focus:**
- Olympic lift variations
- Box Jumps, Med Ball throws
- Speed deadlifts
- Plyometrics

**Conditioning Format:**
- Short, high-intensity intervals
- Sprint work
- Power-endurance circuits

**Rep Ranges:**
- Power: 3-5 sets x 3-5 reps (explosive)
- Conditioning: Short bursts, full recovery

---

### Phase 4: Peak/Maintain (Weeks 25-32)

**Goals:**
- Maintain strength gains
- Skill development
- Active recovery periods

**Strength Focus:**
- Maintain loads from Phase 2-3
- Technical work
- Unilateral movements

**Conditioning Format:**
- Mixed modality
- Sport-specific if applicable
- Fun/varied workouts

---

### Phase 5: Hypertrophy (Weeks 33-40)

**Goals:**
- Muscle building
- Strength-endurance
- Volume accumulation

**Strength Focus:**
- Moderate loads, high volume
- Supersets and giant sets
- Isolation work included

**Rep Ranges:**
- 3-4 sets x 8-15 reps
- Shorter rest periods (60-90 sec)

---

### Phase 6: Active Recovery (Weeks 41-48)

**Goals:**
- Physical and mental recovery
- Address nagging issues
- Maintain base fitness

**Focus:**
- Mobility work
- Light conditioning
- Movement variety
- Outdoor activities

---

## How to Use This System

1. **Select your current phase** based on your goals and time of year
2. **Choose blocks** from each category that match the phase
3. **Assemble your workout** using the template structure
4. **Progress weekly** by adding load, reps, or reducing rest
5. **Deload every 4th week** (reduce volume by 40-50%)
