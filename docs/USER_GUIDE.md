## For Patients

### Patient: Getting Started

**1. Download the App**
- Open TestFlight on your iPhone
- Install "PT Performance"
- Open the app

**2. Sign In**
- Enter the email address your therapist provided
- Tap "Send Magic Link"
- Check your email and tap the login link
- You'll be automatically logged in

**3. Your Home Screen**
After login, you'll see:
- **Today's Session**: Your workout for today
- **History**: Past sessions and progress charts
- **Profile**: Your information and settings

---

### Logging Sessions

**Starting a Session**

1. **Open Today's Session**
   - Tap "Today" tab
   - See your assigned workout
   - Review exercises and target reps/sets

2. **Start Your Workout**
   - Tap "Start Session"
   - Exercises appear one at a time
   - Follow the instructions shown

3. **Log Each Exercise**
   - **Sets**: How many sets you completed
   - **Reps**: Reps per set (e.g., "10, 10, 8")
   - **Weight**: Load used (lbs or kg)
   - **RPE**: Rate of Perceived Exertion (1-10 scale)
   - **Pain**: Any pain during exercise (0-10 scale)

4. **RPE Scale Quick Reference**
   - **1-3**: Very light effort
   - **4-6**: Moderate effort
   - **7-8**: Hard effort, challenging
   - **9-10**: Maximum effort, very difficult

5. **Pain Scale Quick Reference**
   - **0**: No pain
   - **1-3**: Mild discomfort
   - **4-6**: Moderate pain
   - **7-8**: Severe pain
   - **9-10**: Worst pain imaginable

6. **Complete Session**
   - After last exercise, tap "Finish Session"
   - Rate overall session pain (0-10)
   - Add optional notes for your therapist
   - Tap "Submit"

### Viewing Progress

**History Tab**

1. **Pain Trend Chart**
   - Shows your pain levels over time
   - Green = good days
   - Yellow/Red = higher pain days
   - Goal: trending downward

2. **Adherence Chart**
   - Shows how many sessions you've completed
   - Goal: stay above 80% adherence

3. **Past Sessions List**
   - Scroll to see all completed workouts
   - Tap any session to see details
   - Review what you did and how you felt

**Reading Your Charts**

**Pain Trend**:
- **Downward trend** = You're improving! 🎉
- **Flat/steady** = Stable, progressing safely
- **Upward trend** = Talk to your therapist

**Adherence**:
- **Green** (>80%) = Excellent consistency
- **Yellow** (60-79%) = Good, but try to improve
- **Red** (<60%) = Talk to your therapist about barriers

### Understanding Pain

**When to Track Pain**

1. **During exercises**: Rate pain for each exercise
2. **After session**: Overall session pain
3. **Between sessions**: Note any unusual pain

**What Different Pain Levels Mean**

| Pain Level | Meaning | What to Do |
|------------|---------|------------|
| 0-2 | Minimal, acceptable | Continue as planned |
| 3-4 | Mild, monitor | Note in logs, watch for patterns |
| 5-6 | Moderate, caution | Reduce weight or reps |
| 7-8 | High, concerning | Stop exercise, message therapist |
| 9-10 | Severe, alarming | Stop immediately, contact therapist |

**Important**: Some muscle soreness is normal. Sharp, shooting, or worsening pain is not.

### Troubleshooting (Patients)

**Can't find today's session?**
- Check that you're logged in
- Verify with your therapist that they assigned a session
- Pull down to refresh

**Forgot to log a session?**
- Contact your therapist
- They can manually add your session

**App crashed during session?**
- Reopen app
- Your progress should be saved
- If not, message your therapist

**Pain tracking unclear?**
- 0 = no pain
- 5 = moderate discomfort
- 10 = worst pain imaginable
- When in doubt, err on the side of higher numbers

### Creating Programs

**Program Structure**

Programs consist of:
- **Program**: Overall rehab plan (e.g., "8-Week Shoulder Recovery")
- **Phases**: Stages within program (e.g., "Phase 1: Range of Motion")
- **Sessions**: Individual workouts
- **Exercises**: Specific movements with sets/reps

**Step-by-Step Program Creation**

1. **Create Program**
   - Tap "Programs" → "New Program"
   - Enter program name
   - Select protocol (if applicable)
   - Set duration (weeks)

2. **Add Phases**
   - Tap "Add Phase"
   - Phase name (e.g., "Phase 1: Mobility")
   - Duration (weeks)
   - Goals/focus

3. **Add Sessions**
   - Within each phase, tap "Add Session"
   - Session name (e.g., "Day 1: Upper Body")
   - Frequency (e.g., 3x/week)

4. **Add Exercises**
   - Tap "Add Exercise"
   - Search exercise library
   - Set parameters:
     - Sets (e.g., 3)
     - Reps (e.g., 10-12)
     - Rest (e.g., 60 sec)
     - Load (e.g., "Start light, progress weekly")
     - Special instructions

5. **Assign to Patient**
   - Go to patient detail
   - Tap "Assign Program"
   - Select program
   - Set start date

**Using Protocols**

Protocols are pre-built templates:
- **Throwing On-Ramp**: 8-week return to throwing
- **Strength Foundation**: General strength building
- **Post-Op Shoulder**: Post-surgical rehab

To use:
1. Select protocol when creating program
2. Customize as needed
3. Phase constraints are enforced

### Interpreting Flags

**Pain Flags**

| Flag | Trigger | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| High Pain (Red) | Pain ≥7 for any exercise | Review immediately, consider modification |
| Moderate Pain (Yellow) | Pain 5-6 for multiple exercises | Monitor, may need adjustment |
| Pain Increase (Red) | Pain increased >2 points from baseline | Assess cause, modify if needed |

**Adherence Flags**

| Flag | Trigger | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| Low Adherence (Yellow) | <70% session completion | Check-in with patient, identify barriers |
| Missed Sessions (Yellow) | 3+ consecutive sessions missed | Contact patient |

**Workload Flags** (for throwing athletes)

| Flag | Trigger | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| High Workload (Yellow) | Pitch count >threshold | Monitor fatigue, consider rest |
| Velocity Drop (Red) | Velocity down >3 mph | Red flag, assess arm health |

**Responding to Flags**

1. **Review the data**: Click flag to see details
2. **Check session logs**: What happened?
3. **Contact patient**: Get their perspective
4. **Modify plan**: Adjust exercises, load, or frequency
5. **Document**: Add clinical note

### Troubleshooting (Therapists)

**Patient not seeing assigned session?**
- Verify assignment was saved
- Check start date
- Ensure patient is logged in

**Can't create program?**
- Ensure all required fields filled
- If using protocol, check constraints
- Refresh and try again

**Flags not appearing?**
- Check that patient has logged sessions
- Verify data quality (valid pain/RPE values)
- Flags update after session submission

**Charts not loading?**
- Ensure patient has >3 sessions logged
- Check internet connection
- Refresh page

## Common Workflows

### Patient Completes First Session

**Patient**:
1. Opens app
2. Taps "Today"
3. Reviews session
4. Taps "Start Session"
5. Logs each exercise
6. Submits session

**Therapist**:
1. Receives notification (if enabled)
2. Reviews session in patient detail
3. Checks for flags
4. Sends encouragement message (optional)

### Patient Reports High Pain

**Patient**:
1. Logs exercise with pain ≥7
2. Adds note: "Sharp pain in shoulder"
3. Submits session

**Therapist**:
1. Sees red flag on dashboard
2. Opens patient detail
3. Reviews exercise and pain notes
4. Contacts patient
5. Modifies program if needed
6. Documents clinical decision

### Creating Custom Program

**Therapist**:
1. Taps "Programs" → "New Program"
2. Enters "Custom Shoulder Rehab"
3. Adds Phase 1: "ROM" (2 weeks)
4. Adds Session: "Day 1" (3x/week)
5. Adds exercises:
   - Pendulums: 2x20
   - Wall slides: 3x10
   - Isometric holds: 3x30sec
6. Assigns to patient
7. Patient sees session in "Today" tab

## Tips for Success

### For Patients

**Consistency is key**:
- Log sessions right after completing
- Don't wait - you'll forget details
- Be honest about pain and effort

**Communication**:
- Use the notes field
- Tell your therapist if something hurts
- Ask questions during appointments

**Listen to your body**:
- Some soreness is normal
- Sharp pain is not
- When in doubt, stop and ask

### For Therapists

**Set clear expectations**:
- Review pain scale with patients
- Explain RPE at first session
- Show them how to use the app

**Monitor regularly**:
- Check dashboard daily
- Respond to flags quickly
- Provide feedback and encouragement

**Customize thoughtfully**:
- Start conservative
- Progress based on data
- Document your rationale

## Glossary

**RPE**: Rate of Perceived Exertion - how hard the exercise feels (1-10)

**Adherence**: Percentage of assigned sessions completed

**Flag**: Alert indicating something needs attention

**Protocol**: Pre-built program template

**Phase**: Stage within a program (e.g., Phase 1, Phase 2)

**1RM**: One-rep max - maximum weight you can lift once

**RLS**: Row Level Security - database security ensuring patients only see their own data
