# Training Split Options: Full Body vs Upper/Lower

## Quick Takeaways
- Training splits organize exercises across weekly sessions (full-body, upper/lower, body-part splits)
- Research shows split and full-body routines produce similar strength and hypertrophy when volume is equated
- Full-body training (3-4x/week) is optimal for beginners and in-season maintenance
- Upper/Lower splits (4-6x/week) are ideal for off-season when training frequency can be higher
- Baseball players benefit from periodizing splits: upper/lower off-season, full-body in-season

## Introduction

How should you organize your training week? Should you train your entire body each session (full-body split)? Separate upper and lower body (upper/lower split)? Or dedicate entire sessions to specific body parts (body-part split)?

Your training split—how you distribute exercises across weekly sessions—significantly impacts recovery, adherence, and results. For baseball players, the optimal split varies by season phase, training experience, and schedule demands. Recent 2024 research provides evidence-based guidance on this question, showing that properly designed splits produce similar outcomes when total volume is equated—meaning the "best" split depends on your individual circumstances rather than one approach being universally superior.

## Understanding Training Splits

### Full-Body Split
**Definition**: Training all major muscle groups in each session

**Structure**:
- 3-4 sessions per week
- Each session includes lower body, upper push, upper pull, core
- 48-72 hours recovery between sessions
- Moderate volume per muscle group per session

**Example Week**:
- Monday: Full-Body Session A
- Tuesday: Off
- Wednesday: Full-Body Session B
- Thursday: Off
- Friday: Full-Body Session C
- Weekend: Off

**Sample Session A**:
1. Trap bar deadlift 3×5
2. Dumbbell bench press 3×8
3. Single-arm row 3×10 per arm
4. Core work

### Upper/Lower Split
**Definition**: Alternating upper body and lower body focused sessions

**Structure**:
- 4-6 sessions per week
- 2-3 lower body sessions, 2-3 upper body sessions
- Higher volume per muscle group per session
- More exercise variety per muscle group

**Example Week** (4-day):
- Monday: Lower Body A
- Tuesday: Upper Body A
- Wednesday: Off
- Thursday: Lower Body B
- Friday: Upper Body B
- Weekend: Off

**Example Week** (6-day):
- Monday: Lower Body
- Tuesday: Upper Body
- Wednesday: Lower Body
- Thursday: Upper Body
- Friday: Lower Body
- Saturday: Upper Body
- Sunday: Off

### Body-Part Split (Less Relevant for Baseball)
**Definition**: Dedicated sessions for specific muscle groups (chest day, back day, leg day, etc.)

**Structure**:
- 5-6 sessions per week
- Very high volume per muscle group per session
- Each muscle trained once weekly

**Not Recommended for Baseball**: Insufficient training frequency per muscle group, excessive volume per session, poor carryover to athletic performance.

## The Science: Which Split is Best?

### 2024 Meta-Analysis Findings

A systematic review with meta-analysis published in 2024 in the Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research examined split versus full-body resistance training:

**Key Finding**: Split and full-body workout routines promoted similar gains in muscle strength and thickness in upper and lower limbs in untrained individuals. Both training strategies were equally effective during the early training phase (8 weeks).

**Important Caveat**: When total weekly volume is equated (e.g., 15 total sets for quads whether done across 3 full-body or 2 lower-body sessions), outcomes are similar. The split simply determines how volume is distributed.

**Fat Loss Difference**: One 2024 study found full-body resistance training elicited greater fat mass losses compared to split-body training in well-trained males, possibly due to higher total energy expenditure per session.

### Baseball-Specific Research

Research on baseball players specifically suggests split selection should align with season phase:

**Off-Season**: "Split routines during the offseason prove beneficial due to decreased on-field workload, leaving the athlete a greater recovery period between work-bouts allowing for increased volumes and partial body focus."

**Reconditioning Phase**: Studies recommend 4 weekly sessions separated into upper body (UB) and lower body (LB) splits during early off-season rebuilding.

**In-Season Considerations**: "Baseball players play more games than any other sport and need to worry more about strength losses over the course of a long season." This favors full-body maintenance approaches to maximize frequency of muscle stimulation with minimal volume per session.

## Split Selection by Training Phase

### Off-Season (December-February)
**Recommended Split**: Upper/Lower 4-6 days/week

**Rationale**:
- Minimal baseball activities allow maximum training focus
- Higher frequency distributes volume across multiple sessions
- Enables specialization and higher volume per muscle group
- Better recovery between similar movement patterns

**Sample Upper/Lower Split**:

**Lower Body A** (Monday):
- Trap bar deadlift 4×5
- Bulgarian split squat 3×8 per leg
- Leg curl 3×12
- Calf raises 3×15

**Upper Body A** (Tuesday):
- Dumbbell bench press 4×8
- Single-arm row 3×10 per arm
- Overhead press 3×8
- Face pulls 3×15

**Lower Body B** (Thursday):
- Front squat 4×6
- Single-leg RDL 3×8 per leg
- Lateral lunge 3×10 per leg
- Core work

**Upper Body B** (Friday):
- Weighted pull-ups 4×6
- Landmine press 3×8 per arm
- Barbell row 3×8
- Bicep/Tricep work

### Pre-Season (March-April)
**Recommended Split**: Full-Body 2-3 days/week or Upper/Lower 3-4 days/week

**Rationale**:
- Baseball activities increasing significantly
- Need to maintain strength with reduced lifting volume
- Flexibility to adjust based on weekly game/practice schedule

**Sample Full-Body Split** (3 days):

**Monday**:
- Trap bar deadlift 3×5
- Bench press 3×6
- Single-arm row 3×8
- Core

**Wednesday**:
- Bulgarian split squat 3×8
- Overhead press 3×6
- Pull-ups 3×8
- Rotational throws

**Friday**:
- Deadlift variation 3×5
- Landmine press 3×8
- Rows 3×8
- Arm care

### In-Season (April-October)
**Recommended Split**: Full-Body 1-2 days/week

**Rationale**:
- Minimize training frequency due to game demands
- Full-body ensures all muscles stimulated 1-2x weekly
- Time-efficient sessions (30-45 minutes)
- High intensity, low volume maintains strength

**Sample Full-Body In-Season**:

**Tuesday** (Post-game, pre-practice):
- Trap bar deadlift 2×4 @ 85% (high intensity, low volume)
- Single-arm row 2×8 per arm
- Leg curl 2×10 (hamstring protection)
- Face pulls 2×15 (shoulder health)
- Core work

**Friday** (If schedule allows):
- Bulgarian split squat 2×6 per leg
- Dumbbell bench press 2×6
- Pull-ups 2×max
- External rotations

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Full-Body Split

**Advantages**:
- Higher training frequency per muscle (3x/week vs 1-2x/week)
- More flexible scheduling (can miss one session without missing muscle groups)
- Time-efficient for athletes with limited training time
- Easier to auto-regulate based on daily readiness
- Ideal for in-season maintenance

**Disadvantages**:
- Difficult to accumulate high volumes per muscle group
- Longer sessions (need to train everything each day)
- May compromise performance on later exercises due to fatigue
- Less exercise variety per muscle group

### Upper/Lower Split

**Advantages**:
- Higher volume per muscle group per session
- More exercise variety (can do 4-5 quad exercises across 3 weekly lower sessions)
- Better recovery for specific muscle groups (48-72 hours between similar sessions)
- Allows specialization (extra lower body work for pitchers, extra upper for position players)

**Disadvantages**:
- Requires 4-6 weekly sessions (time commitment)
- Missing a session means missing muscle groups for the week
- Not practical during congested in-season schedules
- May create excessive soreness if volume too high

## Choosing Your Split

**Ask yourself**:

1. **How many days per week can I realistically train?**
   - 2-3 days → Full-body split
   - 4-6 days → Upper/lower split

2. **What season phase am I in?**
   - Off-season → Upper/lower split
   - Pre-season → Either (based on schedule)
   - In-season → Full-body split

3. **What are my goals?**
   - Maximize hypertrophy → Upper/lower (allows higher volume per muscle)
   - Maintain strength → Full-body (efficient maintenance)
   - Build work capacity → Full-body (frequent whole-body stimulus)

4. **How's my recovery capacity?**
   - High (good sleep, low stress, no games) → Upper/lower
   - Moderate to low → Full-body (less volume per session)

## Practical Implementation

**Periodize Your Split Across the Year**:
- Weeks 1-8 (Early off-season): Upper/Lower 4x/week
- Weeks 9-16 (Late off-season): Upper/Lower 4-5x/week
- Weeks 17-20 (Pre-season): Full-body 3x/week
- Weeks 21-40+ (In-season): Full-body 1-2x/week

**Use Hybrid Approaches**:
Combine splits based on weekly demands:
- Light week: Upper/Lower 4x
- Heavy game week: Full-body 2x
- Tournament week: Full-body 1x or off

**Adjust Exercise Selection by Split**:
- Full-body: Emphasize compound lifts (limited time)
- Upper/lower: Can include more isolation and variety

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Body-Part Splits for Baseball**: "Chest and triceps Monday, Back and biceps Tuesday" is a bodybuilding approach with poor transfer to baseball. Stick to full-body or upper/lower.

**Ignoring Season Demands**: Trying to maintain an upper/lower 5x/week split during a 6-game week is unsustainable. Match split to reality.

**No Progression Plan**: Randomly alternating splits weekly prevents consistent progressive overload. Stick with one split for 4-8 week blocks.

**Volume Distribution Errors**: Full-body split with 10 exercises per session takes 2+ hours. Keep full-body sessions focused (5-7 exercises).

## When to Seek Professional Help

Consult a strength coach if:
- You're unsure which split matches your training age and goals
- You need help periodizing splits across your season
- You've plateaued with your current split approach
- You're experiencing chronic fatigue or overtraining symptoms
- You want position-specific programming (pitcher vs position player needs)
