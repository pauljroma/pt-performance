# Pre-season Ramp-up: 6-Week Protocol

## Overview

The pre-season phase represents the critical bridge between off-season strength development and competitive performance. This 6-week period transforms raw strength and power into baseball-specific explosiveness while ramping up throwing volume and skill work. A properly designed pre-season protocol peaks athletes at the start of competition while minimizing injury risk.

## The Science of Pre-Season Preparation

Pre-season baseball workout programs typically begin 4-6 weeks prior to tryouts or season start, focusing on improving footwork, hand-eye coordination, flexibility, speed, strength, and endurance [1]. Research supports mid pre-season training programs of 6 weeks with 3 days per week of training, using loads of 80-90% of 1 rep max, performing 4-6 reps for 3-5 sets [2].

The pre-season is when speed, explosiveness, and power use the peak strength developed in the off-season [3]. Training shifts toward power development through explosive lifting with moderate weights moved rapidly and more intensive plyometric training, with medicine ball throws, jump training, and rotational exercises becoming prominent.

## Six-Week Periodization Structure

### Phase 1: Foundation Transition (Weeks 1-2)

**Training Focus:**
- Maintain strength base while introducing power work
- Progressive throwing program initiation
- Movement quality emphasis
- Volume: Moderate-high (transitioning from off-season)
- Intensity: 75-85% of 1RM

**Throwing Progression:**
Build up volume, distance, and intensity over 4-5 active days for 4 weeks before max output throwing [4]. A good general rule is 1 week of on-ramp for every week off from throwing, with on-ramp duration typically being 2-6 weeks [5].

### Phase 2: Power Development (Weeks 3-4)

**Training Focus:**
- Explosive strength and rate of force development
- Increased throwing intensity and distance
- Baseball-specific movement patterns
- Volume: Moderate
- Intensity: 80-90% of 1RM (moved explosively)

### Phase 3: Sport-Specific Peak (Weeks 5-6)

**Training Focus:**
- Maximum power production
- Game-speed throwing and hitting
- Skill integration under fatigue
- Volume: Low
- Intensity: High (85-90% for strength; maximal intent for power)

## Complete 6-Week Training Protocol

### Weeks 1-2: Foundation Transition

**Monday - Lower Body Strength-Power**
- Dynamic warm-up: 10 minutes
- Back squat: 4 x 6 @ 80%
- Romanian deadlift: 3 x 6 @ 75%
- Lateral bound: 3 x 6 each side
- Single-leg RDL: 3 x 8 each leg
- Medicine ball rotational slam: 3 x 6 each side

**Wednesday - Upper Body + Rotational Power**
- Shoulder mobility circuit: 10 minutes
- Bench press: 4 x 6 @ 80%
- Weighted pull-up: 4 x 6
- Landmine press: 3 x 6 each side
- Med ball chest pass: 3 x 8
- Anti-rotation press: 3 x 10 each side
- Arm care circuit: 10 minutes

**Friday - Total Body Power**
- Medicine ball throws (various angles): 4 x 5 each
- Trap bar jump: 4 x 5 @ 40-50% of deadlift max
- Push press: 4 x 5 @ 75%
- Broad jump: 3 x 3
- Core circuit: 10 minutes
- Mobility work: 10 minutes

**Throwing Program (Weeks 1-2):**
- 3-4 days per week
- 60-80 feet maximum distance
- 30-50 total throws per session
- Focus: Mechanics and gradual volume increase
- Intensity: 60-70% effort

### Weeks 3-4: Power Development

**Monday - Explosive Lower Body**
- Box jump: 4 x 4 (near-maximal height)
- Jump squat: 4 x 5 @ 60% of squat max
- Bulgarian split squat: 3 x 6 each leg @ 75%
- Nordic hamstring curl: 3 x 5
- Rotational med ball throw: 4 x 5 each side

**Wednesday - Upper Body Power**
- Med ball overhead slam: 3 x 6
- Explosive push-up: 3 x 6
- Push press: 4 x 4 @ 80%
- Explosive row (bands): 3 x 8
- Landmine rotation (explosive): 3 x 6 each side
- Shoulder health exercises: 3 x 12

**Friday - Sport-Specific Power**
- Rotational med ball throw (multiple angles): 5 x 4 each
- Broad jump to sprint: 3 x 3
- Medicine ball acceleration toss: 3 x 6
- Anti-rotation work: 3 x 30 seconds each
- Conditioning intervals: 6 x 30 seconds sprint, 90 seconds rest

**Throwing Program (Weeks 3-4):**
- 4-5 days per week
- 90-120 feet maximum distance (long toss)
- 50-70 total throws per session
- Focus: Building arm strength, increasing distance
- Intensity: 70-85% effort
- Include: Flat ground throws from pitching distance

**Hitting (Weeks 3-4):**
- Cage work: 5-6 days per week if possible [3]
- Tee work: Quality over quantity
- Live batting practice: 2-3 sessions per week

### Weeks 5-6: Sport-Specific Peak

**Monday - Maximum Power Output**
- Broad jump: 3 x 2 (maximal)
- Power clean or jump squat: 3 x 3 @ 85%
- Single-leg hurdle hop: 3 x 4 each leg
- Med ball rotational throw (maximal effort): 4 x 3 each side
- Sprint mechanics: 4 x 20 yards

**Wednesday - Maintain + Baseball Skills**
- Trap bar deadlift: 3 x 5 @ 85%
- Push-up variation: 3 x 8
- Chin-up: 3 x 6
- Med ball exercises: 3 x 5 each movement
- Extensive baseball skill work
- Mobility and arm care: 15 minutes

**Friday - Peak + Game Prep**
- Medicine ball power circuit: 5 x 3 each exercise
- Sprint work: 5 x 10 yards (acceleration)
- Movement prep and agility
- Light core work
- Extended skill practice

**Throwing Program (Weeks 5-6):**
- 5 days per week
- Full distance (120+ feet for position players)
- 60-80 total throws per session
- Focus: Max intent on select throws, game simulation
- Intensity: 85-95% effort
- Include: Bullpen sessions for pitchers (2x per week)

**Hitting (Weeks 5-6):**
- Daily cage work
- Live batting practice: 3-4 sessions
- Game simulation at-bats
- Video analysis and adjustment

## Recovery and Soreness Management

**Critical Principle:** At any point soreness becomes uncomfortable, rest, then go back and do the previous day again. Do not move forward until the level of soreness lowers [5].

### Recovery Priorities
- Sleep: 8-9 hours nightly (critical for young athletes)
- Nutrition: Adequate protein (1.6-2.2g per kg body weight)
- Hydration: Monitor throughout the day
- Active recovery: Light movement on rest days
- Soft tissue work: Daily foam rolling and stretching

### Weekly Recovery Strategies
- One full rest day per week (no throwing, no lifting)
- Contrast therapy post-high intensity sessions
- Massage or soft tissue work weekly
- Mobility work daily (10-15 minutes minimum)

## Position-Specific Modifications

### Pitchers
- Prioritize arm care and shoulder health exercises daily
- Adjust lifting based on bullpen schedule
- Never lift heavy upper body the day before throwing
- Include thoracic spine mobility work daily

### Catchers
- Additional emphasis on lower body mobility (hips, ankles)
- Extra recovery between heavy squat sessions
- Focus on posterior chain strengthening
- Include hand and forearm recovery work

### Position Players
- Maintain higher training volume than pitchers
- Include sprint work 2-3x per week
- Position-specific movement patterns
- Baseball-specific conditioning intervals

## Monitoring and Adjustment

### Key Performance Indicators
- Throwing velocity (monitor weekly)
- Exit velocity (track in cage sessions)
- Sprint times (10-yard and 60-yard)
- Vertical jump height
- Subjective readiness scores

### When to Adjust
**Reduce volume/intensity if:**
- Throwing velocity decreases by >3-5%
- Persistent muscle soreness beyond 48 hours
- Movement quality deteriorates
- Sleep or appetite disruption

**Increase or maintain if:**
- Metrics improving or stable
- Good recovery between sessions
- High energy and readiness scores
- Clean movement patterns maintained

## Key Takeaways

1. **Progressive overload applies to everything:** Gradually increase throwing volume, distance, intensity, and training load
2. **One week on-ramp per week off:** Use this guideline for throwing progression
3. **Power peaks when strength maintained:** Continue strength work while emphasizing explosive exercises
4. **Skill work increases as strength work decreases:** Shift time allocation toward baseball-specific practice
5. **Listen to your body:** Adjust progression based on soreness and recovery status

## Pre-Season Checklist

- [ ] Establish baseline metrics (velocity, exit velocity, jumps)
- [ ] Create throwing progression schedule
- [ ] Plan strength training around throwing days
- [ ] Schedule 1 full rest day per week

- [ ] Monitor sleep and nutrition
- [ ] Track weekly performance indicators
